In articulatory phonetics, a consonant is a s
peech sound that is articulated with complete or partial closure 
of the vocal tract, except for the h sound, which is pronounced without any 
stricture in the vocal tract. Examples are [p] and [b], pronounced with the 
lips; [t] and [d], pronounced with the front of the 
tongue; [k] and [g], pronounced with the back of the tongue; [h], pronounced throughout the vocal tract;
 [f], [v], and [s], pronounced by forcing air through a narrow channel (fricatives); and [m] and
